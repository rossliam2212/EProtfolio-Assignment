Sound Studio

In our Digital Media module, we have also been learning audio editing. I had a small bit of experience with audio editing prior to college, however I have learned a lot during the Digital Media module. I have been using the application Adobe Audition, which is a digital audio workstation from Adobe Systems, to edit and manipulation audio. I have learned about a variety of different concepts such as noise reduction, pitch, echoing, delay, looping and layering. Below I have some examples of audio edits that I have created.

Pitch & Reverb

For this edit, I started by stretching the song from 100% to 115% to slow it down. I then adjusted the pitch to make the vocals to sound deeper. Lastly, I added reverb and boosted the bass.

8D Audio

My Personal Work

As a personal project, I recently learned how to convert regular songs into 8D songs. 8D songs are stereo tracks which have been edited with spacial reverb and mixing to make it seem like the audio is moving in a circle around your head. Figuring this out was a tricky process, however I was happy with the way the songs turned out. I began the process by adding the "Doppler Shifer" effect to the song. This effect takes advantge of the left and right audio channels to make the song move in a circle. Next I boosted the bass of the song to give it a more amplified sound. I then added studio reverb, which gives the song an echoing effect. Lastly, I enhanced the vocals to make them more clear, as they got washed out after the bass was boosted.
